10.Huawei

Huawei is a very popular smartphone brand known for its high quality and performance. Huawei uses high-end components in its phones, ensuring that each device delivers exceptional speed, power, and durability. The brand is particularly renowned for its advanced camera technology, with many of its smartphones featuring Leica-powered lenses that produce professional-grade photos and videos. Additionally, Huawei invests heavily in research and development, allowing it to introduce innovative features like 5G connectivity, AI-driven performance optimization, and cutting-edge battery technology. While the brand faced challenges with global market access due to geopolitical issues, it continues to dominate in regions such as China and has a loyal customer base worldwide. Huawei’s flagship models, such as the P and Mate series, showcase premium designs, powerful processors, and an excellent user experience, making them a popular choice for tech enthusiasts and professionals alike.


Huawei is a Chinese multinational company founded in 1987 by Ren Zhengfei.

Motorola is currently positioned as the ninth-ranked smartphone brand globally. Motorola smartphones are known for their durability, long battery life, and easy-to-use interface. The company also offers a wide range of features and models catering to various price points, making them accessible to a broad audience. Motorola has consistently focused on providing solid performance with a focus on essential features like display quality and camera capabilities. Their Moto series, in particular, is well-regarded for delivering great value with impressive battery longevity and user-friendly software, making them a popular choice for those looking for a reliable, no-frills device


Motorola, Inc. was an American multinational telecommunications company based in Schaumburg, Illinois. It was founded in 1928 as Galvin Manufacturing Corporation by brothers Paul and Joseph Galvin. The company changed its name to Motorola in 1947.

01.Samsung

Samsung Group[1] (Korean: 삼성; Hanja: 三星; RR: samseong [samsʌŋ]; stylised as SΛMSUNG) is a South Korean multinational manufacturing conglomerate headquartered in the Samsung Town office complex in Seoul. The group consists of numerous affiliated businesses,[2] most of which operate under the Samsung brand, and is the largest chaebol (business conglomerate) in South Korea. As of 2024, Samsung has the world's fifth-highest brand value.


Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul as a trading company, Samsung diversified into various sectors, including food processing, textiles, insurance, securities, and retail, over the next three decades. In the late 1960s, Samsung entered the electronics industry, followed by the construction and shipbuilding sectors in the mid-1970s—areas that would fuel its future growth. After Lee died in 1987, Samsung was divided into five business groups: Samsung Group, Shinsegae Group, CJ Group, Hansol Group, and JoongAng Group.
